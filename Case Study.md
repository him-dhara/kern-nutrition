## The Problem With Most Nutrition Brands

Waaait... have you noticed how every nutrition brand looks identical??

Pastel gradients. Hype copy. "Supercharge your morning." "Fuel your potential."
The same three stock photos of someone holding a green smoothie on a sunny terrace.

It's wellness theater. And busy professionals - the people who ACTUALLY need good nutrition - see straight through it.

They don't have time for rituals.
They don't want a 40-question quiz.
They just want food that works. Quietly. Reliably. Every day.

KERN was built to fix that.

Premium but not cold.
Functional but not clinical.
Honest but not boring.

## The Brand System

Three colors. THAT'S IT. 🙃

| Token | Hex |
|-------|-----|
| White | `#FAFAF7` |
| White - Trans | `#FAFAF7` at 50% |
| White - Trans 20 | `#FAFAF7` at 20% |
| Black | `#111111` |
| Grey | body text gray |
| Ascent | `#688A01` |
| Ascent - 20 | `#688A01` at 20% |

Near-black and paper white do the heavy lifting. Olive green `#688A01` shows up only when something actually needs to stand out - CTAs, stat numbers, key accents. Never decorative.

**Typography:**

| Role | Font | Size | Weight |
|------|------|------|--------|
| H1 | `Dx Ruiga Free` SemiBold | 54px / 1.1 | SemiBold |
| H2 | `Dx Ruiga Free` SemiBold | 45px / 1.1 | SemiBold |
| H3 | `Dx Ruiga Free` SemiBold | 37px / 1.2 | SemiBold |
| H4 | `Dx Ruiga Free` SemiBold | 31px / 1.2 | SemiBold |
| H5 | `Dx Ruiga Free` SemiBold | 26px / 1.2 | SemiBold |
| H6 | `Dx Ruiga Free` SemiBold | 22px / 1.3 | SemiBold |
| Body | `Caladea` Regular | 18px / 1.2 | Regular |
| Button | `Caladea` Regular | 18px / 1.2 | Regular |
| Small | `Caladea` Regular | 15px / 1.3 | Regular |

`Dx Ruiga Free` SemiBold for all headings - sharp, editorial, confident. Holds the near-black palette without competing with it.
`Caladea` Regular for body - readable, neutral, never fights the headings for attention.

### Nav

> *"Products · Science · Teams · Journal"*

Clean minimal nav. Logo left, links center, CTA right.
CTA copy: "Start eating well" - not "Get started." Not "Sign up." Every word earns its place.
Smooth scroll enabled. Placeholder component for the mobile menu.

---

### Hero Section

> *"Nutrition that doesn't ask for your attention."*

Typographic-first hero. KERN wordmark at display scale - the type IS the hero.
Product image floats through the letterforms, Kaiten-style. No lifestyle shots. No hands holding smoothies.

Stat strip underneath: `18 min · 0 ingredients you can't pronounce · 4.8 stars · 6,200 people who eat at their desks`

That stat strip works harder than any headline could.

---

### Features Section

> *"Food that works as hard as you do."*

Ten feature cards. Each with a title, 3-5 keywords, and a 2-line description in pure KERN voice.

- **Clean labels** - If you can't picture it growing somewhere, it's not in KERN.
- **18 minutes** - From fridge to done. Less time than finding parking near the office.
- **No crash** - Low GI across every meal. Your 3pm stays yours.
- **Built for you** - Tell us your week. KERN handles the nutrition math.
- **Cancel anytime** - We don't hide the cancel button. Two taps. You're out.
- **Weekly drops** - Same day, every week. Nothing to remember, nothing to reorder.
- **Real science** - Formulated on research, not what's popular on wellness TikTok.
- **Gut first** - Prebiotic fibre in every meal. Without tasting like it.
- **Team plans** - Feed the whole office without creating a spreadsheet about it.
- **Actually readable** - No superfoods, no hustle fuel. Just food that does what it says.

Every card had a cinematic, grained image prompt generated to match - near-black scenes, olive green tones, paper white light.

## The Copy

Yeppp - every single word on this site was written from scratch. 🙃

Not "placeholder text replaced with real words."

Actually written. With a brand voice. With intention.
With the specific kind of person who lands on this page in mind.

The voice rule for KERN: dry, direct, anti-hype, occasionally funny.
Never says "supercharge." Never says "fuel your potential." Never says "clean eating."
Respects the audience's intelligence. Sounds like a person, not a supplement label.

## The Images

Every image on this site was prompted specifically for KERN's brand world.

Cinematic, grained, moody. Near-black surfaces. Olive green tones. Paper white steam and light.
Generated via Midjourney - video prompts via Veo.

No generic food photography. No lifestyle shots. No hands holding smoothies in golden hour light.
The images had to feel like the brand - not just illustrate it.

And honestly?? That's the only kind of website worth building. 🙃
